Prego no Pao

Description

The Prego no Pão is a simple and delicious Portuguese steak sandwich, available in almost every snack-bar across the country. Like, every Portuguese recipe we have shared so far, they are also associated with a specific town, in this case, Sintra!

The prego, like a bifana is a simple sandwich, consisting of a soft white roll filled with fried meat. In this case it’s a thin slice of beefsteak with lots of fried garlic. It’s not heavily marinated, or particularly saucey, and is usually served with a yellow mustard. Quite popular is to have it com ovo, that’s served topped with a fried egg.

Ingredients

  • 2 white bread rolls
  • 2 beef steaks
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Yellow mustard
  • Salt and black pepper

Preparation

  1. Gently pound the steak to tenderize it (if you don’t have a tenderizer, a rolling pin works well).
  2. Roughly chop the garlic, then place it on top of the meat. Gently pound it again, crushing the garlic into the meat. Season it with salt and black pepper.
  3. Place a medium frying pan over high heat. Once hot, add the butter, olive oil and a bay leaf.
  4. Fry the meat. If using a thin steak, you should need less than 2 minutes on each side.
  5. Remove the steak from the frying pan, reduce the heat to medium.
  6. Cut the bread in half and quickly toast it on the same frying pan you used for the steak.
  7. Spread a good amount of mustard over the bread, place your steak on top. Savour it with your favourite beer!
